Step 2 of 7

Choose the effect metric

For binary outcomes you have three choices. Risk Ratio is interpretable and the default for randomised trials. Odds Ratio is preferred when events are rare or when the comparator log-OR is what your regression model produces. Risk Difference shows the absolute size of effect.

For ATRS (continuous, same scale across studies) you'd pick mean difference. The app handles the conversion to log-units behind the scenes.

Effect measure · binary outcomes
RR Risk Ratio
OR Odds Ratio
RD Risk Difference
Why RR? Trials are randomised so baseline event rates are comparable. RR keeps interpretation simple ("twice as likely") and avoids the rare-event bias of OR.
Step 3 of 7

Pool with random-effects (REML)

Fixed-effect assumes one true effect; random-effects assumes per-study true effects are drawn from a distribution. For multi-centre clinical trials, random-effects is almost always the right call.

The estimator dropdown defaults to DerSimonian–Laird; for ≤20 studies REML with the Hartung–Knapp small-study adjustment gives more honest CIs.

Read the forest plot

Each row shows a trial's point estimate, its 95% CI (the line), and its weight (the square size). The summary diamond at the bottom is the pooled estimate; its width is the 95% CI.

The vertical dashed line is the null effect. For ATRS the pooled MD is 4.6 points favouring early weight-bearing (95% CI 1.8 to 7.4) — statistically convincing but the lower CI sits below MCID (7 points).

Step 5 of 7

Check heterogeneity

I² is the share of variability not explained by sampling error. 25–50% is moderate; above 50% deserves attention. τ² is the between-study variance on the effect-size scale. Q is Cochran's chi-square; its p-value is underpowered with few studies.

The prediction interval is the most honest summary: what would a future trial's effect plausibly land at? Here it crosses MCID — temper your claims.

Step 6 of 7

Funnel plot for publication bias

A symmetric funnel suggests no small-study effects. Visual asymmetry plus Egger's regression p < 0.05 raises concern about publication bias or other small-study effects.

With only 12 trials you're at the threshold for Egger (≥10 minimum) — report the test but caveat it. If concerned, run a trim-and-fill sensitivity that imputes hypothetical missing studies.

Step 7 of 7

Push to your manuscript

One click sends the forest plot into the Manuscript's Results section as an auto-numbered figure, with a pre-drafted caption that carries the pooled estimate (and its citations) inline. The heterogeneity stats, prediction interval, and funnel / Egger / trim-and-fill results stay on the analysis card for you to quote; the forest and funnel also export as PNG or SVG for the figure file.

You'd then edit the prose in the Manuscript editor, switch the bibliography to BJSM style, and head to the Submission tab for the final package.
