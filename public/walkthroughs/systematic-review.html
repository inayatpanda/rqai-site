Running a systematic review from scratch

PICO → search → screen → extract → synthesise → write. Worked example: ACL reconstruction with hamstring vs BPTB autograft.

Step 1 of 8

Start with a PICO

Open the Systematic Review tab. Before anything else, lock down your Population, Intervention, Comparator, Outcomes. Be specific — vague PICOs produce useless searches.

The PROSPERO helper next to the form generates a draft registration block from these fields. You paste that into the PROSPERO portal yourself; the app never registers on your behalf.

Step 2 of 8

Build your search

The Search log holds the queries you'll run in PubMed, Embase, and Cochrane CENTRAL. Write each one with explicit MeSH terms plus free-text synonyms; the MeSH-expansion button pulls in the exploded tree.

Every query is timestamped and the results file is stored next to it so reviewers can reproduce exactly what you ran.

Step 3 of 8

Run searches and deduplicate

Drop each database export into the Library: PubMed XML, Embase .ris, Cochrane .bib are all parsed into the same article shape. Hit Deduplicate — matching is on DOI first, then last-name + year + title-similarity ≥ 0.92.

Expect roughly 28% removed. Your unique-record count is what populates the PRISMA flow's first box.

Step 4 of 8

Screen titles and abstracts

Each record gets three radio actions: Include, Exclude, Maybe. Run two reviewers in blind mode — your colleague's decisions stay hidden until both have voted. Conflicts hit an adjudication queue.

The AI suggestion panel proposes a label using your PICO. Read its reasoning before accepting — especially for Exclude calls. Title-abstract typically reduces 1,260 to ~110.

Step 5 of 8

Full text review with logged exclusions

For each of the 110, click Get full text. The app tries open-access, then your proxy, then prompts for a manual upload. Read inside the Reader tab and highlight outcomes, sample size, follow-up.

Every full-text exclusion must be logged with a PRISMA-compliant reason — that table goes into the supplement. Aim for ~14 RCTs in the final pool.

Step 6 of 8

Extract data into a structured master chart

The Extraction table is pre-seeded from your PICO. For each trial: study ID, country, n randomised, demographics, follow-up, primary and secondary outcome means + SDs, event counts.

A trial that reports a median + IQR (or range) instead of a mean ± SD isn't a dead end: when you build the meta-analysis, switch that study's row to Median / IQR / range and the app converts it to mean ± SD with the Wan (2014) method at run time, so it pools alongside the rest.

Step 7 of 8

Assess risk of bias with RoB 2.0

Open the Risk of Bias card. For RCTs in 2026, use RoB 2.0. The tool picker also offers ROBINS-I (non-randomised), AMSTAR-2 (reviews-of-reviews), and JBI tools.

Five domains, each graded low / some concerns / high. The app stores per-domain rationale and auto-generates the traffic-light figure for your manuscript.

Step 8 of 8

Synthesise and write up

From here, your data feed two outputs. If pooling is appropriate, jump into the Meta-analysis card for forest plots, heterogeneity statistics, GRADE certainty, and Summary-of-Findings tables — see the meta-analysis walkthrough.

If quantitative synthesis isn't appropriate, the Manuscript tab's narrative-review template scaffolds Introduction, Methods, Results, Discussion against the 27-item PRISMA 2020 checklist.
